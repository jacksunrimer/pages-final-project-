History of Nintendo

An interesting backstory on the video game giant company

Starting in 1889...

Early Beginnings


Nintendo, originally titled ‘Nintendo Karuta’ was founded by Japanese craftsman Fusajiro Yamauchi in 1889. Nintendo was founded to sell the popular gambling cards ‘hanafuda’, which were popular at the time. When the market changed, Yamauchi switched to making the cheaper alternative to hanafuda, ‘Tengu.’

Early-Mid 1900s


Later in Nintendo’s career, after the company had exchanged hands throughout the years, the company changed names. ‘Nintendo Playing Card Co.’ contracted with Disney to put the company’s characters on some of their cards in the 1960s, which in part led to the company rising and gaining popularity.

Beginning of Electronic Games


After Nintendo having a tumultuous few years, and selling more conventional games such as chess and mahjong, Nintendo changed its tune to what it is known for today: electronic games. The Laser Clay Shooting System was released by Nintendo in 1973, which was a simulated gun that would shoot light to interact with a target. This was the start of the path Nintendo would take for the next five decades to come!

Nintendo's First Video Game Released


Released in 1977, the ‘Color TV-Game’ is Nintendo’s first video game console to be released. It was one of five home consoles originally sold by Nintendo from 1977 - 1980. It featured six variations of the game pong on it, which they licensed from the creator Magnavox. Despite its success, Nintendo was competing with the big names in the business, Bandai and Tomy, which hurt early video game sales in the late 1970s.

Early 80s


Now that Nintendo was solidified in the up and coming medium of video game consoles, they needed to find their personal flourish. Nintendo brought Shigeru Miyamoto onto their team to lead their next development of arcade games. This led to the creation of the game Donkey Kong, a revolutionary game that allowed much more freedom of the character being played. The player could run left and right, and could jump over obstacles.

Nintendo Takes Off


In 1983, Nintendo launched the family-computer, or ‘Famicom.’ This saw much success in Japan in the early 80s and saw its way to North America with the title NES, or Nintendo Entertainment System. This marked the end of Nintendo’s career as a toy manufacturer and their new strategy of home entertainment through video games. As much as 25% of American households in 1989 owned a Nintendo Entertainment System.

The Wii


Aside from the NES, the Nintendo Wii is the most revolutionary device produced by Nintendo. Released in 2006, the Wii exploded in popularity with its unique design choice. The Wii has a remote that allows the user to interact using their arm, which brought video games to the mainstream and to multiple age groups. The Wii has sold 101 million units, compared to the NES’ 60 million.

Nintendo Today


Nintendo is continuing to revolutionize the gaming industry to this day. The release of the Nintendo Switch in 2017 has proven to be another one of Nintendo’s best selling commodities ever. Nintendo has grown their brand exponentially, and is heavily integrated into Japanese and North American culture. Nintendo has undoubtedly been the most influential company in the video game industry, pioneering the first few generations of video games, while still continuing to innovate. Even 133 years after its founding!
